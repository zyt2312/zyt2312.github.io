Research Experience

DeepResearch RewardBench — Reward-Model Benchmark for Deep-Research Reports 2025 – 2026
Co-first author (2nd listed) · NLP²CT Lab, University of Macau
  • Built the first reward-model evaluation benchmark targeting long-form "deep research" reports, addressing a gap in existing LLM evaluation.
  • Designed and implemented an end-to-end data pipeline: aggregated reports from 19 families of agents/LLMs, scored them under the original evaluation protocol, and through multi-round automatic filtering (scoring consistency, length matching, score-gap constraints) plus PhD-level human verification, constructed pair/triplet/quadruplet preference datasets (331 tasks, 866 reports).
  • Systematically evaluated 17 models from 7B to 235B, designing two metric families (Best-of-N selection and full-ranking consistency); ran analyses on URL robustness, input-order bias, and comparative vs. pointwise scoring paradigms.
  • Distilled a four-category error taxonomy for judge models and operationalized it as a few-shot automatic classifier reaching ~87% agreement with human annotation.
CultMetric — Cultural-Fidelity Evaluation for Classical Chinese Translation 2026
Co-first author (2nd listed) · NLP²CT Lab, University of Macau
  • Proposed a human-free, neuro-symbolic evaluation framework grounded in a glossary of 872 culture-specific items, with five pillars (semantic density, back-translation drift, cultural-anchor stability, asymmetric cultural distance, paraphrase alignment) aggregated by weighted geometric mean for deterministic, reproducible, decomposable scores.
  • Evaluated 4 machine-translation systems on the first five chapters of Journey to the West (1,598 segments per system).
  • Validated discriminative power and independence with Wilcoxon signed-rank tests and Spearman correlation: system-level ranking matched expert judgement exactly, while sample-level correlation with 5 mainstream metrics stayed low (|ρ| < 0.15).
